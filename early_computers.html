CIFA

CIFA is the acronym for "Calculatorul Institutului de Fizică Atomică" (English: Computer of Atomic Physics Institute). CIFA-1, the first Romanian computer, was built in 1957 and made Romania the second socialist country (after USSR) and eighth overall to build an electronic computer.

The logic designs for CIFA-1 started in 1953, at the Academy Physics Institute in Măgurele, with Victor Toma as the head of the project.[1][3] It was presented at the International Symposium in Dresden in 1955, and the prototype, which used 1500 vacuum tubes, a cylindrical magnet memory and machine code programming, was finished in 1957. Its size was that of three wardrobes, it had a paper tape input and a typewriter output and was able of solving 50 operations per second.

MECIPT

MECIPT is the acronym for "Masina Electronica de Calculat a Institutului Politehnic Timisoara" (English: Electronical Computing Machine of the Polytechnical Institute of Timisoara). MECIPT was a typical first generation computer, It contained over 2000 electronic tubes, tens of thousands passive components, words of 30 bits, ss external memory - drum equivalent of 3 kb, data was input into computer by punched paper tape, as a printer was an electrical typing machine.

Its speed was about 50 operations/second and it was programmed in machine code.

Specifications

BUILT IN LANGUAGE Machine language
KEYBOARD binary switches
SPEED 50 instructions per second
BUILT IN MEDIA Tape puncher
